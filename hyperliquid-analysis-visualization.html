Fundamental Issues

The $4M ETH Liquidation Incident

How It Happened:

  1. A whale trader opened a massive 50x leveraged ETH long position worth ~$340M
  2. The trader deposited minimal required collateral
  3. Later, they withdrew $17.09M in margin, deliberately reducing their collateral below maintenance level
  4. This triggered an automatic liquidation of the position
  5. The HLP vault absorbed the position at market price, resulting in a $4M loss
"By withdrawing nearly all of their collateral before the position was liquidated, they managed to avoid slippage and exit the trade without incurring significant losses. However, this strategy shifted the financial burden onto Hyperliquid's liquidity pool (HLP)."
— NFT Evening

Key Vulnerabilities Exposed:

  • Insufficient margin requirements for large positions
  • No limits on position size relative to market liquidity
  • Vulnerabilities in the liquidation mechanism
  • Lack of withdrawal limits for positions nearing liquidation

The $6M JELLY Memecoin Exploit

How It Happened:

  1. Attacker opened two long positions and one short position on the JELLY token
  2. The short position was worth $4.1M, significant for a token with just $25M market cap
  3. Attacker manipulated JELLY price across multiple exchanges, pumping it by 429% in one hour
  4. Due to Hyperliquid's inheritance policies, the HLP inherited the underwater short position
  5. When losses reached $12M, Hyperliquid validators delistedthe token and settled positions at arbitrary price
  6. The attacker withdrew $6.26M of the $7.17M deposited before their withdrawals were frozen
"The attack exploited a vulnerability in how the protocol handled liquidations, pumping the JellyJelly token by 429% in a single hour. Facing the risk of losing everything, the protocol revealed centralized control over market pricing."
— Halborn

Key Vulnerabilities Exposed:

  • Allowing massive positions on illiquid assets
  • Susceptibility to price oracle manipulation
  • Automatic inheritance of positions too large to be liquidated normally
  • Revealed high level of centralization despite claims of decentralization

Solutions Implemented

Enhanced Risk Management & Margin Requirements

Increased Margin Requirements

Minimum margin requirement raised to 20% for certain open positions

Before: Minimal/Variable
After: 20% Minimum

Reduced Leverage Limits

Maximum leverage adjusted downward for major cryptocurrencies

Bitcoin: 50x → 40x
Ethereum: 33x → 25x

Position Size Limits

Implementation of limits on position sizes relative to market liquidity

Before: No limits
After: Proportional limits

Withdrawal Restrictions

Preventing collateral withdrawal for positions near liquidation threshold

Before: Unrestricted
After: Safety thresholds
"Starting March 15, the platform will require a minimum margin of 20% for certain open positions to reduce systemic risks and protect liquidity stability during extreme market fluctuations."
— NFT Evening

Improved Decentralization & Governance

On-Chain Validator Voting

Implemented fully on-chain validator voting system for asset delisting

Enables a quorum of validator stakes to autonomously trigger asset removals directly on-chain without off-chain coordination

Transparent Asset Listing Parameters

Clear criteria established for asset listing/delisting decisions

Formalized rules for market cap requirements, liquidity thresholds, and vulnerability assessments

Enhanced Oracle Systems

Improved price oracle mechanisms to prevent manipulation

Implementation of time-weighted average prices (TWAP) and multiple trusted data sources

User Compensation

Commitment to reimburse affected users from the JELLY incident

Demonstrates commitment to platform integrity and user protection

Lessons & Conclusions

For Traders

  • Reduced maximum leverage still competitive at 40x for BTC and 25x for ETH
  • Greater protections against market manipulation benefit legitimate traders

For Investors

  • Improved risk management framework strengthens long-term stability
  • Enhanced governance moves in right direction but centralization concerns remain

For the Industry

  • Highlights importance of balancing decentralization claims with actual governance
  • Demonstrates need for robust risk management in all DeFi protocols
  • Sets precedent for how DeFi platforms can respond to exploit attempts

Final Assessment

Hyperliquid's swift response to the March 2025 liquidity crises demonstrates the team's commitment to platform stability and user protection. The implemented solutions address the immediate vulnerabilities but raise important questions about the balance between centralization and decentralization in DeFi protocols.

While Hyperliquid maintains its market dominance with 58% share of on-chain perpetual markets, its long-term success will depend on addressing the remaining centralization concerns while continuing to offer competitive features for traders. The introduction of on-chain validator voting for asset delisting is a step in the right direction, but further decentralization of governance will be necessary to fully align with the values of the DeFi ecosystem.
